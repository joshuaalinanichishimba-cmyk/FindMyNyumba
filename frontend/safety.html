Student Safety Center

Find your room without
getting scammed.

Most housing scams in Zambia follow the same handful of tricks. Learn to spot them in seconds, and report anything suspicious straight to our Trust & Safety team.

The golden rule: never send money before you have physically viewed the property. FindMyNyumba never charges viewing fees.

The scams we see most

Recognise the play, and it stops working on you.

"Pay a viewing fee first"

The play: someone asks for K50–K200 on Airtel/MTN Money "to unlock the viewing" or "hold the room". After you pay, they vanish.

What to do: refuse. Real landlords on FindMyNyumba do not charge to view. Report it.

"Send the deposit to secure it"

The play: "Many students want this room — send the deposit now or you'll lose it." Pressure + a personal mobile-money number.

What to do: slow down. Urgency is the scam. View first, then pay only after you have keys or a signed agreement.

"I'm the landlord" (they're not)

The play: someone lists a property they don't own — often a real building they photographed or copied from another site.

What to do: check for the Verified Landlord badge. Ask to meet at the property. No badge + won't meet = walk away.

The "agent" fee

The play: a middleman demands a non-refundable "agent fee" before he'll even show you a place, then disappears or shows nothing.

What to do: you don't need a paid agent to find a room here. Report anyone demanding fees upfront.

Spotting a fake listing

Fake posts share tell-tale signs. If two or more of these line up, treat it as suspicious.

  • Price too good to be true. A self-contained near CBU at half the going rate is bait.
  • Photos look too polished or are watermarked from another site — they may be stolen.
  • The "landlord" won't meet or is "always travelling / abroad".
  • They push you off-platform to WhatsApp immediately and avoid in-app chat.
  • Money before viewing, in any form, for any reason.

Reading the badges

Every listing and landlord carries a trust badge. Here's what each one means.

Verified Landlord Identity (NRC + selfie) reviewed and approved.
Verified Property Photos, location and documents checked by our team.
Phone Verified Phone confirmed, identity not yet reviewed. Be cautious.
Identity Submitted Documents uploaded, under review.
Unverified Nothing confirmed. Never send money to an unverified user.

A safe viewing, step by step

Follow these and you cut your risk to almost nothing.

1

Chat in-app first

Keep messages on FindMyNyumba. Our system watches for scam patterns there.

2

Check the badge

Prefer Verified Landlord / Verified Property listings. Ask why if there's no badge.

3

View in daylight

Go in person, ideally with a friend. Confirm the room, the address and who you're dealing with.

4

Pay only after

Money changes hands once you've viewed and have keys or a signed agreement — never before.

Report fraud

Saw something off? Tell us.

Reports are confidential and go straight to our Trust & Safety team. Even if you weren't scammed, flagging a suspicious listing protects the next student.

In immediate danger or a victim of theft? Contact the Zambia Police on 991 as well.
